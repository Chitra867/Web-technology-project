Chhath – Devotion to the Sun God

Chhath is a major Hindu festival observed in Nepal’s Terai region and parts of India. It is dedicated to the Sun God (Surya) and his consort Chhathi Maiya. The festival honors the power of the sun as the source of energy and life on Earth.

Spanning four days, Chhath includes holy bathing, fasting, standing in water to offer argha (prayers), and rituals performed with utmost devotion. Devotees pray to the setting and rising sun on the banks of rivers and ponds.

Chhath rituals promote discipline, purity, and environmental respect. Offerings of fruits, sweets, and thekuwas are carried in bamboo baskets and placed on banana leaves or soopas, creating a serene and spiritual atmosphere.

The festival strengthens community ties and reflects deep-rooted Vedic traditions. Its visual beauty and spiritual energy make Chhath one of the most revered festivals in Nepal’s cultural calendar.
